🏔️ Exploring the Hidden Kingdom: Mustang, Nepal

July 20

Tucked away in the rain shadow of the Himalayas, Mustang feels like a land forgotten by time. Often called the "Last Forbidden Kingdom," this region blends raw natural beauty with deep Tibetan-Buddhist culture. From the windswept cliffs and mysterious caves of Upper Mustang to the apple orchards and ancient monasteries of Jomsom and Kagbeni, Mustang offers a surreal escape from the modern world.

🕊️ Swayambhunath: The Timeless Watcher of the Kathmandu Valley

July 20

Perched atop a hill overlooking Kathmandu, Swayambhunath Stupa — lovingly called the Monkey Temple — is one of Nepal’s most sacred and iconic religious sites. Believed to be more than 2,000 years old, it’s a spiritual beacon for both Buddhists and Hindus, symbolizing peace, wisdom, and awakening.

🗻 Annapurna – The Goddess of Harvests and Himalayas 🗻

July 20

Annapurna is more than just a mountain — she’s a symbol of power, beauty, and life. Towering at 8,091 meters, Annapurna I is the 10th highest peak in the world and one of Nepal’s greatest treasures. The name “Annapurna” comes from Sanskrit, meaning “Giver of Food and Nourishment.” From breathtaking views to spiritual significance, she represents both challenge and grace — feared by climbers, worshipped by pilgrims. She stands tall not just in height, but in the heart of every Nepali. 🇳🇵❤️ .
